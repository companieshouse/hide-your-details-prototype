Removing your personal information from the Companies House public register

How to remove your personal details or protect your identity from appearing on the Companies House public register

Contents

  • – Removing personal information from the public register
  • – Protecting your identity on the public register
  • – Reporting that a company has used your personal information without your permission

Most information registered at Companies House is available to the public.

Other personal details, such as your home address and full date of birth, are not published on the Companies House register. This information is only shared with:

  • credit reference agencies (CRAs)
  • specified public authorities (SPAs) such as the police

Your home address will only be shown on the public register if you have used it as your correspondence address, or as a company's registered office address.

Removing personal information from public documents

If you've used your home address as your correspondence address, or as a company's registered office address, you can ask Companies House to remove it from publicly available documents on the register.

You'll need to know which documents contain your home address. Check this by finding your company on the Companies House register.

If your home address is currently being used as the company's registered office address, you'll need to submit a filing to update the registered office address we hold on the public register first.

Apply to remove your personal details from Companies House public documents

Protecting your details on the public register

If you're at serious risk of violence or intimidation, you can apply to protect your details from appearing on the public register.

Protecting your details means:

  • none of your information will be shown on the public register
  • we will not provide your home address to CRAs

You can apply for protection if you (or someone living with you) are at personal risk, or if you're at risk because of your connection to a company or LLP.

You'll need to be able to prove that you are at serious risk of violence or intimidation. If possible, you should provide evidence to support your application.

Read the full guidance on applying to protect your details

Reporting that a company has used your personal information without your permission

If a company has given your personal details to Companies House without your permission, you need to let us know. This includes is a company has used your name or address.

We can usually remove your information from the Companies House register if there’s enough evidence to support your case.
