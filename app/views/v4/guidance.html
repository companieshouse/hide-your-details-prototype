Removing your personal information from the Companies House public register

How to remove your personal details from public documents, or protect your identity from appearing on the Companies House public register.


Most information registered at Companies House is available to the public.

Your home address will only be shown on the public register if you have used it as your correspondence address, or as a company's registered office address.

Other personal details, such as your full date of birth, may be included on historical documents on the public register.

Removing personal information from public documents

You can ask Companies House to remove some personal details from previously filed documents on the public register. This includes your:

  • home address
  • signature
  • full date of birth (we'll still show your month and year of birth, but the day will be removed)
  • business occupation

You'll need to know which documents contain these personal details. You can check this by finding the company on the Companies House register and searching its filing history.

If your home address is being used as a company's current registered office address, you need to submit a filing to update the registered office address before you can apply for it to be removed from historical documents.

Protecting your details on the public register

If you're at serious risk of violence or intimidation, you can apply to protect your details from appearing on the public register.

Protecting your details means:

  • none of your information will be shown on the public register
  • we will not provide your home address to credit reference agencies

You can apply for protection if you (or someone living with you) are at personal risk, or if you're at risk because of your connection to a company or limited liability partnership (LLP).

You'll need to be able to prove that you are at serious risk of violence or intimidation. If possible, you should provide evidence to support your application.

Read the full guidance on applying to protect your details


If a company has used your personal information without your permission

You need to report if a company has given your personal details to Companies House without your permission. This includes if a company has used your name or address.

We can usually remove your information from the Companies House register if there’s enough evidence to support your case.
